Threats to turtles





Threats to turtles Description
Habitat loss and degradation Destruction of nesting beaches, pollution, and coastal development reduce suitable habitats for turtles.
Climate change Rising temperatures affect sex determination in some species, leading to skewed sex ratios and negatively impacting population sustainability.
Pollution Accumulation of plastic debris, oil spills, and chemical pollutants in water bodies harms turtles through ingestion or entanglement.
Illegal trade Capture and trade of turtles for their meat, shells, or as exotic pets contributes to population decline and threatens their survival.
Bycatch Turtles unintentionally get caught in fishing gear, particularly in trawls, gillnets, and longlines, resulting in injury or death.
Invasive species Predation by invasive predators, such as rats and feral pigs, can have detrimental effects on turtle nests and hatchlings.
Overharvesting Unsustainable hunting of turtles for food, traditional medicine, and cultural purposes poses a significant threat to their populations.
Disease Outbreaks of diseases, such as fibropapillomatosis, can weaken turtles' immune systems and lead to increased mortality.
