Leatherback sea turtle

Leatherback sea turtles are the largest living turtles on Earth, reaching up to seven feet in length and weighing up to 2,000 pounds. These remarkable creatures have been around for over 100 million years, with their ancestors dating back to the time of the dinosaurs. Leatherbacks are known for their unique leathery shell, which lacks the bony plates found in other turtle species. They are also distinct for their ability to regulate their body temperature, which allows them to thrive in both warm and cold waters.Leatherbacks can be found in every ocean on the planet, with some on beaches in the tropics.

Olive Ridley turtle

The Olive Ridley turtle is a species of marine turtle known for its olive-colored shell. Found in warm oceans, they are relatively small, with adults measuring about 2 feet in length and weighing around 75 to 100 pounds. Olive Ridley turtles exhibit arribada nesting behavior.They are omnivorous, consuming jellyfish, shrimp, crabs, and seagrasses.These turtles face threats such as habitat destruction, entanglement in fishing gear, and poaching. Conservation efforts focus on protecting nesting beaches, implementing fishing regulations, and raising awareness. Preserving Olive Ridley turtles and their habitats is essential for their survival and the ocean's biodiversity.

Loggerhead sea turtle

The loggerhead sea turtle is a species of marine turtle recognized for its large size and robust appearance. They inhabit various warm and temperate waters around the world, including the Atlantic, Indian, and Pacific Oceans. Named for their oversized head, loggerheads have a reddish-brown carapace and powerful jaws. Adults can reach lengths of about 3 feet and weigh up to 300 pounds or more. Loggerhead sea turtles are known for their strong nesting instincts, often returning to the same beaches where they were born to lay their eggs. They primarily feed on a diet consisting of bottom-dwelling invertebrates, such as crabs, mollusks, and sea urchins.

Hawksbill sea turtle

The hawksbill sea turtle is a remarkable species of marine turtle recognized for its unique appearance and ecological significance. Found in tropical and subtropical waters around the world, they have a distinctive narrow, pointed beak resembling a hawk's bill, which gives them their name. Hawksbill turtles possess a stunningly beautiful carapace characterized by a pattern of overlapping scales that resemble tortoiseshell, making them highly sought after for their attractive shells. However, this has unfortunately led to their endangerment due to illegal trade. These turtles play a crucial role in coral reef ecosystems, as they feed primarily on sponges, helping to maintain the health and balance of coral reefs.
